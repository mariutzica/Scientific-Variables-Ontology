ice

matter

Definition: Ice is water frozen into a solid state, typically forming at or below temperatures of 32 °F, 0 °C, or 273 °K. Depending on the presence of impurities such as particles of soil or bubbles of air, it can appear transparent or a more or less opaque bluish-white color.
